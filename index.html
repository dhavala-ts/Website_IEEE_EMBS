Design and Development of Wireless Flexible Electronics Nasal Wiflen Mask for respiratory breathing assessments

About

Aim: To develop a flexible wireless electronics mask comprehensive breath monitory and respiratory assessment

Expected outcomes-


1.Prior reviewed Journal / Conference Publication.
2. Development of different Machine Learning and Deep Learning models for analysis and visualizations of breath signals.
3.Mobile Application displaying respiratory vitals and the type of breathing data.


Overview
The project focuses on evaluating respiratory signals to identify four breathing patterns: normal, deep, rapid, and shallow. It utilizes a wearable nasal mask with humidity sensors to collect data, which is then preprocessed by removing baseline drift and outliers. The data is normalized and segmented for analysis. An ensemble model combining 1D-CNN, LSTM, and SVM classifiers is employed for classification. The framework's effectiveness is measured using accuracy, F1 score, and Matthews Correlation Coefficient (MCC).

Publications




The Wireless Flexible Electronic Nasal (WIFLEN) sensor, paired with a Fast Dense CNN (FASDEN-CNN), captures temperature, pressure, and humidity at the nostrils to recognize breathing patterns. After collecting and cleaning data from various activities, feature extraction and statistical analysis were performed. A deep AI model was developed to classify inhalation and exhalation points, validated against a clinical device. Significant differences in nostril measurements across activities were noted, and Hilbert transform was applied to generate 2D spectrograms for binary classification using FASDEN-CNN.

The respiratory signal assessment framework uses a wearable nasal mask to capture temperature, pressure, and humidity variations from both nostrils during normal breathing, deep breathing, and walking. This method, using a nasal sensor instead of traditional thoracic sensors, cleanses and segments the signals with a 10-second Hamming window at 50% overlap. The normalized segments are analyzed using the EDRS-LSTM deep learning model, with performance evaluated on precision, recall, ROC, accuracy, and F1 score for the three breathing conditions.

The evaluation framework for respiratory signals analyzes four breathing patterns: normal, deep, rapid, and shallow. A wearable nasal mask with humidity sensors collects data, which is preprocessed to remove baseline drift and outliers. The data is normalized and segmented with 80% overlap. An ensemble model combining 1D-CNN, LSTM, and SVM classifiers is used for analysis. Performance is assessed using accuracy, F1 score, and Matthews Correlation Coefficient (MCC).
